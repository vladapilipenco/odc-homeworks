Easy Grilled Octopus Recipe

The Greeks have been eating octopus since ancient times, and it's still on the menu of the country's many taverns. On the islands, where the catch is often fresh, octopus is grilled over charcoal, seasoned with fresh lemon. If you've never tried it before, you should know that octopus can be notoriously tough and rubbery. The first question most people ask is how to get the octopus truly tender. If you tried to grill the octopus from raw stage, you'd end up with the most awfully chewy tentacles, so the meat needs a little help before it hits the grill. The TRULY old school Greek fisherman tenderizing method is to bash the octopus against some seaside rocks, but I'm going to assume you don't have that kind of rock access, and lord knows there are cleaner ways to go about it. Boiling is the most common way to soften the meat, and that's the step I've found most successful.

Ingredients

  • 1 Octopus (3-4lbs)
  • 20 cups Water
  • 2 tbsp Salt
  • I Lemon cut in half
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 2 sprigs Thyme
  • 2 sprigs Parsley
  • 1/2 cup Olive oil

Instructions

  1. Use half of the lemon and wash the octopus
  2. Use all the other ingredients except the oil create a broth
  3. Bring the broth to boil for about 5
  4. Dip the tentacles in the water for about 10 seconds to help then create and maintain the curl
  5. Boil octopus for about 50-75. Depending on size
  6. When octopus is tender, turn off water and drain pot
  7. Place octopus on a cutting board or platter and cut in 4 sections. Please not that octopus can stay overnight in a refrigerator before grilling
  8. Using the olive oil and your favorite herb, create a base, and toss in octopus
  9. Grill for about 15 minutes
  10. Remove octopus from grill and slice as desired
